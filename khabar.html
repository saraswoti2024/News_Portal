Govt trying to gloss over poor performance: UML boss Oli

Taking part in the debate on the government's policies and programmes in the House of Representatives today, Oli said that the government was acting like Nero who played the flute while Rome was burning. He said the government's document was full of contradictions. In point 5 of the government's document, the government says that things have improved significantly and the economy is doing alright, but in point 14, the government says that economy is in crisis, Oli added.Stating that there was no plan to achieve high rate of economic growth, Oli said there was no vision for achieving the goals of social justice. Policy and programme document lacks vision, objective and goal, he added.The former PM said that economic indicators pointed that the country's economy was facing recession, but the government has not even realised this fact and it has not told the public about it. He said that the prime minister sought help of the international community to collect revenue. "How can the government do that? Is it not the government's primary priority to collect revenue? The government has failed in its primary first duty" he said.He said the government had achieved more than 6 per cent economic growth in 2017, 2018 and 2019, but that growth rate came down to an abysmal two per cent due to the mistakes of Nepali Congress and CPN-Maoist Centre coalition.

DMG issues licenses for exploration of 420 mines

It has issued this many permits for the exploration of possible mines so far as of the current fiscal year, said Narayan Banskota, the Department's Information Officer. Banskota said licenses have been provided for exploration of iron ore, copper, gold, limestone, granite, ruby, dolomite, among other minerals.The Department had issued licenses for exploration of 405 mines. It has given permit for quarrying 159 mines until the current fiscal year and for quarrying 158 mines.The Department said permit has also been given for opening a mine for the exploration of iron ore at Dhaubadi of Nawalparasi.The Department has identified 50 mines so far and issued licenses to different companies for their operation
